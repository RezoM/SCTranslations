Accessing Decoderland™

To access Decoderland™, tap the scanner button in the navigation area of the main screen.

Decoderland™ supports three input sources: camera, still image, and keyboard input. By default, the camera input is active. You can switch to the still image or keyboard input using the buttons in the toolbar at the bottom of the camera screen. To access a specific source directly, swipe the scanner button slightly downward — a pop-up menu with all three sources will appear.

You can also choose what happens when you tap the scanner button by setting your default action in the app settings.

Supported data types

Product and Shipment Identification

  •  GTIN-13  - Identifies retail products using a 13-digit Global Trade Item Number. Supports also 8-, 12- and 14-digit codes. Commonly found on packaging worldwide.
  •  ITF-14  - Identifies shipping containers, cartons, and outer packaging with a globally unique trade item number in a scannable format. Commonly used in logistics and chain tracking supply.
  • ISBN - International Standard Book Number. Used to uniquely identify books and other monographic publications.
  • ISMN - International Standard Music Number. Used to uniquely identify editions of printed or digital notated music.
  • ISSN - International Standard Serial Number. Used to uniquely identify serial publications such as magazines, journals, and periodicals.
  • Tracking Numbers Identifies numbers used for shipping, logistics, and product tracking (e.g., UPC, FedEx, USPS).
  • SSCC - Serial Shipping Container Code used to identify logistics units
  • NDC - US “National Drug Code”

Personal and Contact Information

  •  vCard / meCard  -  Digital contact cards that store names, phone numbers, and emails. Instantly add a contact by scanning.
  •  Phone  -  A direct phone number. Scan to make a call or save to contacts.
  •  Email  -  Encodes email address, subject, and message. Opens your email app with a pre-filled draft.
  •  SMS  -  Contains a phone number and message. Opens your messaging app ready to send.

Finance and Payments

  • BIC / SWIFT   - Identifies banks for international transactions.
  • IBAN  -  International Bank Account Numbers. Used for cross-border payments and transfers.
  • SEPA  - Single Euro Payments Area transfer format. Decodes payment data like IBAN, BIC, amount, and recipient.
  • SwissQR  - A special QR code that encodes payment information in a standardized way so invoices can be scanned and processed by banks and accounting software.
  • Credit / Debit Card Numbers -  Decodes a card number for verification or transactions.
  • ISIN  - The International Securities Identification Number — an international standard code that uniquely identifies a security globally for the purposes of facilitating clearing, reporting and settlement of trades.
  • CUSIP  - An alphanumeric code to identify a specific security, such as a stock or bond.

Travel and Transportation

  • VIN Codes -  Unique codes for identifying vehicles. Decodes manufacturer's.
  • Boarding Passes -  Used by airlines for check-in. Scanning retrieves flight and passenger information.
  • Baggage Tags -  Issued by airlines to track luggage.
  • MRTD / MRZ -  Machine-readable zones from passports, national IDs, US Green Cards, visa stickers etc. Scanning extracts official personal and travel details.
  • Vehicle registration plates (Number plates) for some countries.

Network and Connectivity, IT

  • WiFi -  Includes network SSID, password, and encryption type.
  • IP address lookup – Maps an IP address to a geographic location, providing estimated information such as country, region, city, time zone, and provider.
  • UUID - Universally Unique Identifier.
  • Snowflake ID - A form of unique identifier used in distributed computing.

Web and Location Links

  • URL  - Web address in barcode form. Shows site preview and opens the site in your browser.
  • Location  - Geographic coordinates. Opens a map app to show the specified place.

Multimedia

  • ISRC - The International Standard Recording Code. Is used for uniquely identifying sound recordings and music video recordings.
  • ISWC - The International Standard Musical Work Code is a unique identifier for musical works.
  • GRid - The Global Release Identifier (GRid) is a system to identify releases of digital sound recordings (and other digital data) for electronic distribution.
  • ISAN, V-ISAN - The International Standard Audiovisual Number is a unique identifier for audiovisual works and related versions.

Calendar and Events

  • iCalendar / ICS Events - Decodes event title, date, time, location. Scanning can add the event to your calendar.

ID and Regulatory Codes

  • AAMVA / ANSI - Driver’s License and ID Card. Decodes complete set of all encoded tags.
  • ORCID - An Open Researcher and Contributor ID used to uniquely identify authors and contributors of scholarly communication.
  • DOI - A digital object identifier or handle used to uniquely identify various objects.
  • URN - Uniform Resource Name. Decodes some types on URN namespaces.
  • SSN - US Social Security number.

Inline Parsing

The Inline Parsing feature allows you to quickly decode codes without searching manually.

How it works:

  • Type desired code.
  • Decoderland™ will attempt to identify and decode it immediately.

Supported code systems include:

  • International Organization for Standardization (ISO) country codes:
    • ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 (e.g., /US → United States)
    • ISO 3166-1 alpha-3 (e.g., /USA → United States)
    • ISO 3166-1 numeric (e.g., /840 → United States)
  • Federal Information Processing Standard (FIPS) 10-4 country and region codes (e.g., /US34 → New Jersey)
  • Global Standards 1 (GS1) country codes (e.g., /301 → France)
  • International Telecommunication Union (ITU) country codes (e.g., /E → Spain)
  • ITU telephone country codes (E.123) (e.g., /34 → Spain)
  • ITU mobile country codes (MCC) (e.g., /262 → Germany)
  • North American Numbering Plan (NANP) area codes (e.g., /575 → Las Cruces, New Mexico, US)
  • International Olympic Committee (IOC) country codes (e.g., /POR → Portugal)
  • International Federation of Association Football (FIFA) country codes (e.g., /NED → Netherlands)
  • International Union of Railways (UIC) country codes (e.g., /10 → Finland)
  • American National Standards Institute (ANSI) U.S. state and territory abbreviations (e.g., /CT → Connecticut, US)
  • ISO 639 language codes — 2- and 3-letter forms (e.g., /zu → Zulu)
  • ISO 15924 script codes (e.g., /Deva → Devanagari)
  • ISO 4217 currency alphabetic codes (e.g., /GBP → British Pound)
  • ISO 4217 currency numeric codes (e.g., /978 → Euro)
  • Airline codes: IATA (e.g., /BA → British Airways), ICAO (e.g., /BAW → British Airways), and numeric airline codes
  • Airport codes: IATA (e.g., /JFK → John F. Kennedy International Airport), ICAO (e.g., /KJFK → John F. Kennedy International Airport)
…and more coming soon.

Currency conversion

Decoderland™ can recognize valid numbers as amounts and show currency conversion rates for your selected currencies. This works with all input types: camera, still image, and keyboard. To activate the converter from the keyboard, just type a space (or tap return key) followed by amount.

Tap the left or right side of the converter frame to choose the currency you want. On the manual input screen, a long press lets you set your own conversion rate.

You can also control the converter’s behavior in the app settings.
