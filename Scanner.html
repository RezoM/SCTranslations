What is ISSN-13
ISSN is an eight-digit code, divided by a hyphen into two four-digit numbers. The last digit is a check digit. ISSNs can be encoded in EAN-13 bar codes with a 977 prefix, followed by the 7 main digits of the ISSN (the check digit is not included), followed by 2 publisher-defined digits, followed by the EAN check digit. Unlike the ISBN code, ISSN is an anonymous identifier associated with a serial title, containing no information as to the publisher or its location.
Purpose of ISSN-13
To uniquely identify a periodical publication globally under the GS1/EAN system.
Abbreviation Meaning
An “International Standard Serial Number” (since 1976).
Where It's Used
  • Periodical publication
  • Libraries
  • Publishing industry
